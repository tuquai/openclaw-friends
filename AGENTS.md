## Design Context

### Users
OpenClaw Friends serves broad consumer users who want to create believable AI companions and character personas without wrestling with complex configuration. They are typically exploring emotional companionship, creative role design, or lightweight identity prototyping for Discord and OpenClaw workflows. The interface should help them move from curiosity to confidence quickly, while keeping the experience approachable enough for first-time users and polished enough for commercial public deployment.

### Brand Personality
The brand should feel high-end, intimate, and future-facing. It should evoke trust, emotional warmth, and the desire to create. The tone should be confident and refined rather than loud, playful, or trend-chasing. The product should feel like a premium consumer experience, not an AI wrapper, internal tool, or hacky builder.

### Aesthetic Direction
The visual direction is premium emotional companionship in a warm light palette, with both light and dark themes planned from the start. The strongest reference is Apple.com for clarity, restraint, and product-level polish, but adapted toward softness and intimacy instead of cold hardware minimalism. The experience should not look like a generic AI product, dashboard template, or decorative glassmorphism-heavy interface. The UI should feel calm, trustworthy, and commercially deployable, with a subtle sense of future technology beneath a human, welcoming surface.

### Design Principles
1. Build trust through clarity: every screen should feel ordered, legible, and intentionally structured, with strong hierarchy and minimal clutter.
2. Lead with emotional premium quality: use warm, soft surfaces and elegant spacing to create intimacy without becoming cute, noisy, or childish.
3. Make creation feel aspirational: the interface should make users feel they are crafting something meaningful and high-value, not filling out a generic form.
4. Avoid AI-wrapper aesthetics: no generic neon gradients, shallow chatbot tropes, or interchangeable SaaS admin patterns.
5. Ship commercial-grade polish: interactions, responsive behavior, accessibility basics, and theme consistency should be good enough for a public production launch.
